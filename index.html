A fairer, healthier, and better understanding of addiction is within reach.
But only if we are willing to know and empathize of their struggles
Discovering addiction's complexities is like exploring different landscapes in our minds. It's a deviation influenced by diverse factors. From stress storms to learned scripts, this narrative experience unveils how individuals cope and navigate towards resilience and recovery.

Chapter 1 EXPLORING THE DARK
A JOURNEY INSIDE THE MIND OF ADDICTION
YOU TRIED IT ONCE.
Perhaps at a party, given to you by a friend,
or maybe you got it at your own will

And so you search
for that taste again
Longing for its
intoxicating charm



And you try it again, and again, and again

You chase that high
to satisfy your now
unsatisfiable desire
for it



Yet that light at the end of the tunnel that you
so tirelessly chase never gets any closer,
no matter how hard you run for it.

























There is no
single personality trait
that can lead to addiction.


Rather, addiction
is rooted in various
characteristics and traits.
People who suffer from addictive behaviors generally show the following personality traits:
Open to new experiences or adventurous in nature
Low conscientiousness or low levels of mindfulness and control
Disagreeableness or not conforming to social rules
Neuroticism or emotional instability and moodiness

Chapter 2 THE TUG OF WAR
Addiction alters the physical characteristics and functioning of the brain.
01
The reason why people become addicted to something has mainly to do with pleasure produced by these behaviours.
02
These pleasurable experiences are directly linked to the inner workings of the brain, particularly with neurotransmitters.
03
Neurotransmitters are like messengers, they carry signals or messages from one part of the brain to the other.
04
Drugs alter what kind of neurotransmitter is sent and the quantity sent.
05
One particular neurotransmitter most commonly associated with addiction is dopamine.
06
Dopamine is a neurotransmitter that plays a role in motivation and pleasurable rewards.
07
It alters connections in the brain that makes it easier to repeat a behaviour over and over again.
08
This leads to habits and may end up as an addiction
